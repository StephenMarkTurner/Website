Strength Training Outline

  • Strength training is strong medicine. You need to use the right amount. Fatigue and minor aches and pains can accumulate. Usually the best plans involve an ebb and flow (they are periodized).
  • I divide the exercises into 3 main categories. Upper body, Lower body, and Core (including Neck and Grip). Sometimes the 3 categories are folded into two for a higher frequency program. I also use full body sessions, especially after a rest phase. The sessions typically get harder (in some manner) through a work phase of about 18 workouts in 24 days, followed by a rest phase (only gentle exercise) for a full 6 to 7 days.
  • I like a variety of weight, rep, set and rest ranges. I time some of the sets, for example doing a set every 1 to 3 minutes, or using a specific time to work, and also to rest. A new month can be a good time to change these things up.

Examples of Monthly Breakdowns

I aim for about 18 workouts in about 24 days, then end of month break. Many partitions are possible, here are just a few examples. Don't overcomplicate this. It's not that important. Some have the rest days more distributed, others have fewer, longer rests. You may prefer one style over the other, or changing it up.Try some, or invent your own.
