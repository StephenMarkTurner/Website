A Lesson In Correlation And Causation
Or...
My Arm! It hurts Where The Tiger's Biting It!

It seemed simple enough.

A few years ago I went out for a bicycle ride in the morning. I had a modest breakfast consisting of a glass of fruit smoothie and some oatmeal and peanut butter, and set out about an hour later. I rode at a moderate intensity for about 3 hours. When I returned home I was feeling hungry, so I went out for a typical fast food lunch. About an hour or so after eating I felt quite ill, and proceeded to forcefully purge the food I had eaten.

Lesson learned, right? Or was it confirmation bias?

The following weekend I went out again for a slightly longer ride. I had the same, but larger, breakfast. The plan was to avoid the temptation to get any fast food afterwards. I rode for about 2 hours and was ready to turn around and return home, and that was when I started sensing that nauseated feeling again. I had a very unpleasant ride home, in fact I was actually sick several times mid ride.

Unfortunately, the illness continued for over a week. I finally thought that I would change breakfast and see if that had any effect. To my surprise, it did! It took another week for the symptoms to completely dissipate. I then decided to try the ingredients from my original breakfast one at a time.

Bingo! It seemed to be the goji berries that were in my smoothie. Not the greasy fast food that I first suspected.

All in all, a terrific but quite painful lesson. I had briefly contemplated an emergency visit to the hospital (yes, the symptoms at times were that bad). Although I still eat a highly plant centric diet, I stay well clear of goji berries.
